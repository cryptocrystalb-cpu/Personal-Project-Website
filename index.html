My love for desserts started back in 8th grade, when I dreamed of creating my own cakes with fondant, cookies, and other sweet treats that stood out from the ordinary. Over the years, I experimented with recipes like Oreo cake, chocolate chip cookies with marshmallows, and white-and-dark chocolate chip cookies. Each creation fueled my excitement to keep learning and growing. One of the most memorable experiences was helping my godmother in the kitchen of the art museum in South Carolina. She was preparing cupcakes and desserts in elegant cups for an event, and she invited me to join her. On my very first try at icing and preparing cupcakes, I discovered how natural it felt—and how much joy it brought me to contribute. My godmother, who built her own dessert business and connected with so many people through her craft, inspired me to pursue my own path in baking. I’ve always wanted to be great at making desserts like her, but in my own unique way.

I created this website to share that passion with the world. To me, desserts are more than food—they’re art, comfort, and joy. Through step-by-step demonstrations, photos, and clear instructions, I want to empower people who may feel intimidated by baking. This site is a learning hub where anyone can gain confidence, whether they’re making simple cookies or elaborate cakes. Each recipe is a canvas where I combine flavors, visuals, and storytelling, inviting others to join in the creativity. My goal is to spread joy by showing how easy and fun it can be to make something sweet. I believe desserts can brighten someone’s day, encourage them to try something new, and remind them of life’s simple pleasures. Over time, this website also becomes a way to build my personal brand—showcasing my creativity and expertise, opening doors to collaborations, sponsorships, or even a future cookbook. Most importantly, this website transforms my passion into possibility. It’s the first step toward something bigger: workshops, a dessert-focused business, or simply a community of dessert lovers who inspire each other. Food brings people together, and desserts often take center stage in celebrations. By sharing my journey here, I hope to build a space where we can connect, learn, and celebrate the art of desserts together.

25 Cookie Recipes

Each recipe includes ingredients, timing, and clear steps.

1) Chocolate chip cookies
Prep 15 min Bake 10 min Yield ~24
  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ¾ tsp fine salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, room temp
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  1. Heat oven to 375°F. Line two sheet pans.
  2. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Cream butter and sugars until light, 2–3 minutes.
  4. Beat in eggs one at a time.
  5. Mix in dry ingredients just until combined.
  6. Fold in chocolate chips.
  7. Scoop 1½ tbsp portions, 2 inches apart.
  8. Bake 9–11 minutes until edges are golden.
  9. Cool 5 minutes on pan, then on racks.
2) Oatmeal raisin cookies
Prep 20 min Bake 12 min Yield ~24
  • 1¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, room temp
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 cups old-fashioned oats
  • 1 cup raisins
  1. Heat oven to 350°F. Line pans.
  2. Whisk flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. Cream butter and sugars; add eggs.
  4. Mix in dry ingredients, then oats.
  5. Fold in raisins.
  6. Scoop; flatten slightly.
  7. Bake 11–13 minutes until set at edges.
  8. Cool on racks.

7) Gingerbread cookies
Chill 1 hr Bake 8–10 min Yield ~40
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp cloves
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup molasses
  1. Whisk dry spices with flour and salt.
  2. Cream butter and sugar; add egg, molasses.

  4. Roll ¼-inch; cut shapes.
  5. Bake 8–10 minutes at 350°F.
8) Butter shortbread
Prep 10 min Bake 25–30 min Yield 1 pan
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup rice flour or cornstarch
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  1. Heat oven to 325°F.
  2. Beat butter, sugar, vanilla until creamy.
  3. Mix in flours and salt.
  4. Press into lined 9-inch pan; dock with fork.
  5. Bake 25–30 minutes until lightly golden.
  6. Cut while warm; cool fully.

16) Brownie cookies (crackly)
Prep 20 min Bake 10–12 min Yield ~24
  • 1 cup dark chocolate chips
  • ¼ cup butter
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • ¾ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup cocoa powder
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  1. Melt chocolate and butter; cool slightly.
  2. Beat sugar and eggs until thick, 2 minutes.
  3. Fold in melted chocolate and vanilla.
  4. Mix in sifted dry ingredients.
  5. Rest batter 10 minutes; scoop.
  6. Bake 10–12 minutes at 350°F.

Baking basics and troubleshooting

Quick fixes for spread, texture, and doneness.

Spread too much

Chill dough 30–60 min, use parchment, and ensure butter is just soft, not melted.

Too cakey

Reduce flour slightly; avoid overmixing; use more brown sugar for chew.

Underdone centers

Bake 1–2 min longer or cool on pan to carryover—edges should be set, centers just soft.

Inconsistent batches

Weigh ingredients, rotate pans halfway, and keep dough portions uniform.
